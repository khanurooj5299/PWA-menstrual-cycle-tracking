Don't wash your hair or take a bath when you're menstruating:

There is absolutely no reason not to wash your hair, or shower during menstruation. In fact, a nice warm bath can do a lot to relieve menstrual cramps and premenstrual tension.

You need to wash your pads before throwing them out. Otherwise ghosts will come and haunt you:

There is absolutely no need to do so. Disposable pads should be discarded after use. Ghosts don't have time to hunt used pads.

Periods are shameful, dirty and impure:

Women on their periods are not dirty or unclean. Being on your period is not something to be ashamed of or to be kept a secret. In fact we should try to talk more openly about periods so that we get proper sanitation facilities and correct information.

You can't do exercise and should only rest when you're on your period:

It is a myth that exercising can worsen period cramps. There is no scientific reason for a woman with a normal cycle to stop exercising while she's on her period.

Sour foods can worsen cramps:

It is essential for women to maintain a healthier diet during periods, but there is no connection between menstrual cramps and eating sour foods.
